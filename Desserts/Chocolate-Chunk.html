Brown Butter Chocolate Chunk Cookies

Recipe Details

  • Prep Time: 25 minutes
  • Cook Time: 12 minutes
  • Total Time: 3 hours (includes chilling)
  • Servings: 18 Cookies

🛒 Ingredients

  • 1 cup (2 sticks) unsalted butter
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • ¾ teaspoon kosher salt
  • ¾ cup packed dark brown sugar
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg + 1 egg yolk, room temperature
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 8 ounces bittersweet chocolate, roughly chopped
  • Flaky sea salt, for sprinkling

👩‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Brown the Butter: Melt butter in a saucepan over medium heat. Whisk constantly until it turns a deep golden brown and smells nutty (about 5-8 minutes). Transfer to a bowl and let cool slightly.
  2. Dry Mix: Whisk together flour, baking soda, and salt in a medium bowl.
  3. Wet Mix: Combine cooled brown butter with both sugars. Add the egg, yolk, and vanilla; whisk vigorously until the mixture looks pale and smooth.
  4. Combine: Gently fold the dry ingredients into the wet mixture until just combined. Fold in the chocolate chunks.
  5. Chill: For the best flavor, chill the dough for at least 2 hours (or overnight).
  6. Bake: Preheat oven to 350°F. Scoop 2-tablespoon sized balls onto a lined baking sheet. Bake for 10-12 minutes until edges are golden.
  7. Finish: Sprinkle immediately with flaky sea salt. Cool on the pan for 5 minutes before moving to a wire rack.

📝 Notes

Flavor Secret: Browning the butter adds a rich, toffee-like depth that regular butter can't match. Don't skip the chilling step—it prevents the cookies from spreading too thin!
